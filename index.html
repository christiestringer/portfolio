About

When choosing my subjects for year 12 at Saint Kentigerns, I found myself at a crossroads. Having already chosen French and Accounting, I was stuck for my third option. My father suggested that I chose Computer Science, as I had ways had a “knack for computers”. Five years on and I am eternally grateful to my father for his input. While my French vocabulary still needs some work and my love for balance sheets never really came to fruition, I discovered a true passion for coding, an interest that I would love to develop into a career.

Along with this interest in coding, I have long been fascinated by the marketing industry. For this reason, I decided to pursue a conjoint degree of Computer Science and Marketing. After two years of study at Victoria University, I am currently undertaking my third year at Auckland University. Over summer, I took part in a tech internship at Healthlink, a health system integrator that enables medical practices to communicate electronically with the rest of the health sector. This was an eye opening experience that exposed me to the realities of working in the tech industry and really confirmed my desire to pursue a career in this area.

While I am most interested in developing my skills in front-end development, I am curious about all fields of development, and eventually hope to become a full stack developer. Google is renowned as being the leading employer in this regard, and a company that values curiosity, passion and a desire to learn. I would relish the opportunity to work with innovative colleagues who think big and tackle challenges from a fresh perspective. My aim is to work in a environment with a highly involved and dynamic working culture, and I consider that Google would provide an atmosphere that is conducive to personal growth.

Restaurant-Location Bot

This TwitterBot is a small project I worked on to develop my skills in Javascript, NodeJS, NPM, APIs and also git. Although small, and unlikely to be the "next big thing", it was a great side project to work out to develop my personal skills.
To start with I did some research on creating a TwitterBot and watched a few tutorials. I started off by creating the bot to do a simple task such as tweeting out a random tweet when someone sent the account a tweet. From here, I went through the Yelp API and played around with this to get the information that I needed.
The next step was the put that information into a tweet to return to the user. This was a bug that was difficult to fix, as there was an issue with returning information from one function to another due to the Yelp API. This took me a while to fix, but finally managed to fix by finding the information I needed in the first function and bypass needing to call the second one.
Another challenge that I had to overcome was understanding git. Git is a very useful tool, and I intend to use it with all projects from now on, however the initial learning curve for it was quite steep, and I am still currently climbing it. There were several times where I lost commits, or pushed or pulled something I shouldn't have, however, eventually I managed to get my project up and running online, with the help of Git.

What I enjoyed most about creating this application was seeing it live in action, most of the projects I work on are for university papers, and they don't go past the marker's desk. But this project is live and running and it is something that I am quite proud of, no matter how small.
